# Kiaros MVP Cut v1

_Date: 2026-04-09_

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the **first real version of Kiaros** that should be built, tested, and potentially sold.

Its purpose is to protect the product from overbuilding, protect the founder from trying to price the entire future vision at once, and identify the smallest version of Kiaros that still delivers real magic.

This is not the final form of Kiaros. It is the first strong container.

---

## 2. Core Promise

**Kiaros helps people plan their lives in alignment with their goals, timing, and natural cycles instead of forcing themselves through generic productivity systems.**

More simply:

**A personalized planning system built around your goals, timing, and natural cycles.**

This means the first version must help a user feel:
- understood
- guided
- less pressured by linear planning norms
- more connected to what season they are in
- more able to make real decisions in the present

If the product does that, it is valuable even without the full future roadmap.

---

## 3. Primary Launch User

### Primary user for v1
A spiritually curious or self-development-oriented woman who is burned out by generic planning systems and wants a more personalized, cyclical, meaningful way to organize her life.

### Traits of this user
- already somewhat familiar with astrology, cycles, self-reflection, or alternative wellness frameworks
- frustrated by planners that assume everyone should operate the same way
- wants guidance that feels personal and relevant, not generic
- is emotionally open to reflective and symbolic frameworks
- wants structure without rigidity
- wants to feel permission to rest, pivot, and plan more intelligently

### Why this user first
This user is the clearest fit for Kiaros’s current strengths and messaging. She is the most likely to immediately understand the value without requiring too much explanation.

---

## 4. What Kiaros Must Do in v1

Kiaros v1 does **not** need to do everything in the full product bible.

It **does** need to create one real and convincing experience:

> “This system took my life seriously, understood where I am, and gave me a better way to move through my time.”

To accomplish that, v1 must do four things well:

1. **Capture meaningful context**
2. **Generate a personalized plan / blueprint**
3. **Translate that plan into near-term guidance**
4. **Provide enough interaction that the user can keep using it in real life**

---

## 5. Kiaros MVP Must-Haves

## A. Guided Onboarding
The onboarding must feel thoughtful and personal, not bureaucratic.

### Must include
- birth data
- current goals / life categories
- current year or season vision
- optional cycle integration
- recent-life reflection (important for rolling-entry use)

### Why recent-life reflection belongs in MVP
Kiaros should not behave like a January-only planner or a generic astrology app.

It should be able to meet a user where they are now.

This means onboarding should include some combination of:
- what has been happening over the last 2–3 months
- what has felt easy vs hard lately
- where they feel stuck, stretched, or called forward
- a few tailored reflection questions that make the system feel interpretive rather than formulaic

### MVP standard
By the end of onboarding, the system should have enough information to feel like a personalized mirror — not just a birth-chart generator.

---

## B. Blueprint Generation
This is the hero artifact of Kiaros v1.

### Must include
- a personalized seasonal / yearly / current-period overview
- core themes
- timing guidance
- push / build / maintain / rest framing
- guidance connected to the user’s own goals and words
- language that feels grounded, specific, and useful

### MVP standard
The blueprint must feel like:
- “this is about me”
- not “this is generic astrology text with my name on it”

### Important note
The blueprint does **not** need to cover every possible layer of interpretation in v1. It just needs to feel coherent, personal, and actionable.

---

## C. Weekly / Monthly Guidance Surface
Users need somewhere to actually use the blueprint.

### Must include
- an overview/dashboard
- current week guidance
- current month context
- visible energy framing (push / build / maintain / rest / integrate)
- a practical view of what matters now

### MVP standard
A user should be able to log in and quickly understand:
- what season they are in
- what to prioritize
- what not to force
- how current timing relates to their larger goals

### Important note
This surface does not need every advanced calendar interaction from the full product spec. It just needs to be good enough to support real weekly use.

---

## D. Light Interpretation / Guidance Layer
The system should not feel static.

### MVP options
Either of these is acceptable for v1:
1. **Light Oracle** — simple guided AI interpretation/chat with narrower scope
2. **Contextual interpretation blocks** — pre-generated explanations attached to current week/month guidance

### Why this matters
A live interpretation layer makes the product feel:
- responsive
- personalized
- contemporary
- supportive in the moment

### MVP standard
The user should be able to get help making sense of their current timing without requiring the entire future Oracle feature set.

---

## 6. Strongly Recommended but Optional for First Beta
These are valuable, but they do not need to block launch if the core experience is already strong.

### Optional for first beta
- simple daily check-in
- basic note field or micro-journal
- simple transit highlights
- basic quick-log of energy / mood
- lightweight save / revisit functionality for blueprint segments

These can improve stickiness, but they are not the heart of the first value test.

---

## 7. Post-MVP Features
These are the features that meaningfully improve retention and richness after the core product has been validated.

### Post-MVP candidates
- richer calendar views
- deeper daily tracker
- tracker metric customization
- stronger Oracle with more grounded context
- journaling experience
- progress reflection features
- saved history / versioning improvements
- better personalization over time
- clearer pattern feedback loops

### Why these are post-MVP
These features deepen the product, but they are not required to test whether the fundamental promise lands.

---

## 8. Later Delight / Expansion Features
These are valuable and beautiful, but they are not required to prove Kiaros deserves to exist.

### Later delight / expansion
- quarterly reviews
- Year Unwrapped
- share cards
- beautiful data visualizations across the whole year
- advanced ritual journaling features
- extensive settings panels
- polished viral moments
- highly sophisticated analytics and pattern surfacing
- deeply expanded Oracle behavior

### Important reminder
These features are not “bad to cut.”
They are “good to earn later.”

---

## 9. Explicitly Not Building Yet
This section exists to reduce founder drift.

Kiaros v1 is **not** trying to be all of the following at once:
- a full annual review engine
- a complete custom habit tracker
- a fully mature journaling platform
- a giant astrology encyclopedia
- a full social sharing product
- a full quantified-self system
- a complete all-seasons polished operating system on day one

If a feature does not directly support the first believable transformation, it should be considered suspicious until proven necessary.

---

## 10. The First Transformation Being Sold
Kiaros v1 is not selling “all possible future value.”

It is selling something more specific:

> A more personalized, cyclical, and meaningful way to understand what season of life you are in and how to move through it.

Or even more directly:

> Instead of forcing yourself through a generic plan, Kiaros helps you see where you are, what matters now, and how to work with your real timing.

That is enough to be valuable.

---

## 11. Why This MVP Is Still Worth Paying For
A focused MVP is still worth paying for if it:
- saves emotional energy
- reduces confusion
- makes planning feel more personal and less oppressive
- helps users make better decisions about timing and effort
- creates a feeling of being understood
- offers a meaningful alternative to generic productivity systems

### Founder grounding note
The price does not need to justify every future feature.
The price only needs to honor the value of the actual experience being delivered now.

---

## 12. Founder Constraints and Guardrails

### Known founder risk
When pricing feels emotionally difficult, the founder tends to add more features to justify charging.

### Corrective guardrail
Before adding a feature, ask:
- Does this improve the first transformation meaningfully?
- Does this help the user trust and use the system now?
- Or am I adding this because I feel guilty charging?

If the answer is guilt, fear, or worth-proving, the feature should probably wait.

### Grounding principle
**Overbuilding is not evidence of value.**
It is often evidence of pricing discomfort, fear of judgment, or difficulty receiving.

---

## 13. What Makes Kiaros Sellable in Beta
Kiaros becomes sellable in beta when:
- the onboarding feels personal
- the blueprint feels real
- the near-term guidance feels useful
- the product clearly reduces overwhelm or confusion
- the user leaves with language like:
  - “I feel seen”
  - “This gave me clarity”
  - “This feels different from other astrology apps”
  - “I actually understand what to focus on now”

That is enough to support beta pricing.

---

## 15. Working Summary
Kiaros v1 should focus on a small but real promise: helping people plan in alignment with their goals, timing, and natural cycles through a personalized onboarding flow, a meaningful blueprint, and practical near-term guidance.

The first version does not need to contain the entire dream. It needs to create a strong enough experience that people feel understood, guided, and relieved by it.

This cut exists to protect the magic by giving it a shape small enough to build, test, price, and release.
